Push Pull Leg Routine

A push-pull-legs (PPL) gym split is a popular and effective workout routine designed to target all major muscle groups over three workout sessions. This split allows for balanced training, sufficient recovery time, and versatility in exercise selection. Here's a detailed description:

Push Day:

Focus: Chest, shoulders, and triceps

On push day, the exercises are focused on the muscles involved in pushing movements. This typically includes:

  • Chest Exercises: Bench Press (Barbell or Dumbbell), Incline Bench Press, Chest Dips, Chest Flyes
  • Shoulder Exercises: Overhead Press (Barbell or Dumbbell), Lateral Raises, Front Raises, Rear Delt Flyes
  • Triceps Exercises: Tricep Dips, Tricep Pushdowns, Overhead Tricep Extensions, Skull Crushers

Pull Day:

Focus: Back and biceps

On pull day, the exercises target the muscles involved in pulling movements. This typically includes:

  • Back Exercises: Pull-Ups or Chin-Ups, Bent Over Rows (Barbell or Dumbbell), Lat Pulldowns, Seated Cable Rows, Deadlifts
  • Biceps Exercises: Barbell Curls, Dumbbell Curls, Hammer Curls, Preacher Curls
  • Rear Deltoid Exercises: Face Pulls, Reverse Pec Deck Flyes

Legs Day:

Focus: Quads, hamstrings, glutes, and calves

On legs day, the exercises focus on the lower body muscles. This typically includes:

  • Quad Exercises: Squats (Barbell or Dumbbell), Leg Press, Lunges, Leg Extensions
  • Hamstring Exercises: Deadlifts (Romanian or Stiff-Legged), Hamstring Curls, Good Mornings
  • Glute Exercises: Hip Thrusts, Glute Bridges, Step-Ups
  • Calf Exercises: Standing Calf Raises, Seated Calf Raises

Weekly Schedule:

The PPL split can be arranged in various ways depending on your training frequency. A common approach is:

  • Three-Day Split: One session each of push, pull, and legs per week.
    • Monday: Push
    • Wednesday: Pull
    • Friday: Legs
  • Six-Day Split: Each session is repeated twice per week, with one rest day.
    • Monday: Push
    • Tuesday: Pull
    • Wednesday: Legs
    • Thursday: Push
    • Friday: Pull
    • Saturday: Legs
    • Sunday: Rest

Benefits of PPL Split:

  • Balanced Muscle Development: Ensures all major muscle groups are targeted evenly.
  • Efficient Recovery: Allows muscle groups adequate recovery time before being worked again.
  • Flexibility: Easily adjustable to different training frequencies and goals.
  • Variety: Allows for a wide range of exercises, reducing workout monotony and enhancing motivation.

Arnold Split Routine

The Arnold split routine is a classic and highly effective bodybuilding program designed by Arnold Schwarzenegger. This split focuses on hitting each muscle group twice a week, allowing for intense and frequent training. Here's a detailed description:

Day 1: Chest and Back

Focus: Chest and back muscles

  • Chest Exercises: Bench Press, Incline Bench Press, Dumbbell Flyes, Dips
  • Back Exercises: Pull-Ups, Bent Over Rows, T-Bar Rows, Deadlifts

Day 2: Shoulders and Arms

Focus: Shoulders, biceps, and triceps

  • Shoulder Exercises: Overhead Press, Lateral Raises, Front Raises, Rear Delt Flyes
  • Biceps Exercises: Barbell Curls, Dumbbell Curls, Preacher Curls
  • Triceps Exercises: Skull Crushers, Tricep Dips, Overhead Tricep Extensions

Day 3: Legs and Lower Back

Focus: Quads, hamstrings, glutes, and lower back

  • Leg Exercises: Squats, Leg Press, Lunges, Leg Extensions, Hamstring Curls, Calf Raises
  • Lower Back Exercises: Deadlifts, Good Mornings, Hyperextensions

Weekly Schedule:

The Arnold split is typically structured over a six-day training week with one rest day. Here's an example schedule:

  • Monday: Chest and Back
  • Tuesday: Shoulders and Arms
  • Wednesday: Legs and Lower Back
  • Thursday: Chest and Back
  • Friday: Shoulders and Arms
  • Saturday: Legs and Lower Back
  • Sunday: Rest

Benefits of Arnold Split:

  • High Volume Training: Emphasizes high volume and frequency, leading to significant muscle hypertrophy.
  • Balanced Muscle Development: Ensures all muscle groups are trained twice a week, promoting balanced growth.
  • Intensity: Allows for intense workouts that can lead to substantial strength and size gains.
  • Variety: Includes a wide range of exercises, keeping workouts engaging and challenging.
